We Russians living in Japan came to this country for different reasons. Some of us wanted to embrace a different culture, some came because of the job opportunities, some simply couldn’t stand the ideology of the current Russian government. We cannot be silent. The horrors of war committed against Ukraine by the Kremlin are despicable, a tragedy.

We are Russians. We never wanted this war. We do not support the invasion of Ukrainian cities such as Luhansk, Donetsk and Kherson. We do not condone nor recognize the referenda of the occupied territories. It is hard to imagine that a government unable to provide fair elections to its own people, would do so during on foreign lands at war.

We condemn the actions of the Russian government. We do not want Russians to be portrayed as supporters of the Putin dictatorship. We wish world to see us as allies. Honest people who wish only for world peace.

We are terrified due to the recent news regarding partial mobilization on the territory of Russia. All of Putin’s claims are deceitful manipulations meant to protect his power. Russians are not cannon fodder to be thrown away at this pointless war. People have lives, families and beliefs. Mass protests have erupted since the announcement of the mobilization. We stand in full disagreement with the actions of the Russian government and we wish to protect our families and loved ones in Russia.

We raise our voices, demanding the release of Alexei Navalny, who was imprisoned for protecting Russian citizens and moving against Putin's regime. When interacting with his lawyer, Navalny is not allowed to make eye contact. A glass sealed with opaque tape seperates them. His lawyer cannot hand over documents for inspection. Other inmates are forbidden to even take a look at Navalny. He is constantly being sent into solitary confinement for benign reasons such as an undone button on hist shirt or he saying that people are not toy soldiers. We are terrified. Navalny's safety is in jeopardy. Through his story, we want to remind people of the horrors committed against civilians held hostage by Putin.

We are deeply grateful to all Japanese citizens who joined our protests and helped with their organization. We appreciate that Japan has given us the freedom to express our feelings through peaceful protests, as freedom of expression is a human right we are denied in our homeland.

We believe that Japan should allow entry to Russians who do not want to fight in Putin’s army. Every man able to find a safe haven in Japan leads to multiple human lives are saved. These men, regular men, not soldiers, would have been forced to fight Putin's war and face the impossible choice of sacrficing their lives or taking others'. For the sake of peace, we hope that Japan will show empathy and offer solutions in these times of need.
